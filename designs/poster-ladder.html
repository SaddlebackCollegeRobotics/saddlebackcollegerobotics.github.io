(2018), doi: 10.1089/ast.2017.1773.  •  Likelihood — A summary of the factors to the right including specificity, ambiguity, false positive, false negative and detectability.  •  False Positive — A positive determination based on detection due to contamination or a positive detection caused by an unintended interference with the method of detection.  •  False Negative — Lack of detection when the factor is actually present either in the sample or the system at large. For example, low signal to background or signal below the limit of detection.
